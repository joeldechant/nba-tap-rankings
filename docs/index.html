TED — Total Earned Differential

TED estimates total player production per game as a single points-equivalent number. It relies primarily on box score stats, converting all box-score contributions — points scored, scoring efficiency, rebounds, assists, steals, turnovers, blocks — along with a defensive adjustment (using DBPM and DWS) into one value. TED is meant to capture a player’s full impact on points scored in a game, both directly and indirectly — his Total Earned Differential. For example, if a player scored 30 points with a TED of 52 in last night’s game — he actually contributed 52 points worth of total offensive/defensive production for his team across all facets of the game, not 30. TED is normalized to per 36 minutes and 71 possessions for cleaner cross-player and cross-era comparisons.

TAP — Total Adjusted Production

TAP estimates total player production per game as a single points-equivalent number. It builds on TED (Total Earned Differential), which converts all box-score contributions — points scored, scoring efficiency, rebounds, assists, steals, turnovers, blocks — plus a defensive adjustment (using DBPM and DWS) into one value. TAP takes this approach one step further, overlaying an additional offensive adjustment (using OBPM and OWS) to capture the residual offensive impact that box-score stats miss — for example, shooting gravity that warps defenses, or anti-gravity. TAP is normalized to per 36 minutes and 71 possessions for cleaner cross-player and cross-era comparisons.
